  • You will see a series of images, each one for a very brief time. Each image will feature a single object from 64 possibilities. The objects are common things you might see around your house, on TV, in books, or on the Internet.

  • After you see an object, you must set the slider to indicate how well a given adjective describes it, on a scale of 0-100. 0 indicates the adjective does not describe the object well at all, and 100 indicates the adjective describes it very well.

  • After seeing each image, click anywhere on the slider bar to set the slider location to the number you like. You can click to drag the slider around, or you can click anywhere on the bar again to reset the slider position. Click "Next Trial" to continue to the next object.

  • For example, if you saw a picture of a car and saw the adjective "boxy", you would think about how "boxy" a car is and set the slider accordingly. We also provide the dictionary definition of every adjective, which you should read if you are unsure of what a word means.

  • Even if you're not 100% sure of what you saw, make your best guess. Even if the adjective seems unusual for the given object, try your best to set a reasonable value.

  • When you have worked though all 100 images, this HIT will submit itself automatically.
